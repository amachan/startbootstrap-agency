Evans Lake

User Experience Design, Responsive Website Redesign

Client: Evans Lake
Completed: June 2017
Team: Amanda Chan, UX Designer, Sherry Xie, UX Designer, Kirk Zhang, UI Designer

View the full case study:

Brief

Evans Lake is a camp and outdoor forest education facility that is also available for private events. There is a lot of good content on the existing website, but it needs to be streamlined and better organized. They also want to improve on the experience for those viewing information on smaller devices.

Goals

  • Make the website reflect the core offerings of the organization
  • Increase click through from homepage of the website
  • Reduce the amount of content
  • Improve navigation
  • Update look and feel

Skills

Domain Research
User Research
User Interviews
Information Architecture
Prototyping (Paper & Digital)
User Testing

Research

Since we were unable to pull any analytics off the existing website, we focused our research on understanding the different user groups and the domain of kids camp. Through a survey and interviews we discovered people’s favourite aspects of Evans Lake and top factors they considered when deciding to choose Evans Lake for a private event or kids camp. With this information we created two personas representing parents who send their kids to camp and someone looking to book the venue.

Design & Testing

Improving the information architecture was one of our top priorities. We started off reviewing the existing content. Based on our research and personas we created a new structure and tested this through one on one closed card sorting with our users. From there we were able to finalize a site map that the majority of people were able to navigate. We then did a design studio to develop ideas on how to display large amounts of content found on the website. Read more about the design decision behind each component in my case study. Although we were able to decrease the number of pages from 48 to 29, there were still many pages to be made. Kirk took the lead UI role and Sherry and I assisted in creating some pages based on the style guide that Kirk developed with the client.

Deliverables

  • Persona
  • High-fidelity prototype (InVision)
  • Digital wireframes (Sketch)
  • Client presentation

The design was chosen to be developed by the RED Academy Web Developer Professional students. We provided them with documentation and the assets to create the site on wordpress and also worked with them to resolve any issues with the design feasibility.

GoGram App

User Experience Design, Mobile iOS App Design

Client: GoGram
Completed: May 2017
Team: Amanda Chan, UX Designer, Catherine Doxford, UI Designer, Elliot Mayer, UX Designer

View the full case study:

Brief

GoGram is a video editing service that focuses on making professional quality video editing fast and easily accessible at a lower price point than other services. Currently, video submissions are accepted on GoGram’s website and they return the edited video in under 24 hours. GoGram wants to extend their services to be offered on smartphones as a mobile app to make it even more accessible. The app is then expected to become the primary source of video submissions.

Goals

  • Increase the number of accounts and video submissions on GoGram
  • Communicate that videos are edited by people instead of algorithms
  • Ensure customer retention
  • Increase engagement

Skills

User Research
User Interviews
Prototyping (Paper & Digital)
User Testing

Research

GoGram is a new startup that is in the validation stage. They had limited research into their users, so we helped to identify them and further understand what their needs and motivations were through user interviews and surveys. Gathering and organizing this information through an affinity diagram, we were able to create a persona of our primary user. I also did a comparative/competitive analysis and created a positioning map to better understand its competitors and how the company differentiates itself.

Design & Testing

Since we were creating a new mobile app for the GoGram service, we identified a list of features and prioritized them to determine the minimum viable product. From this we created the site map and user flow to guide our designs. We did a design studio and took our best work into a paper prototype. We iterated our design through rounds of user testing with 9 individuals. From our medium-fidelity prototype Catherine worked with us to create the high-fidelity prototype. To view some feedback we received from user testing, check out my case study.

Deliverables

  • Persona
  • High-fidelity prototype (InVision)
  • Digital wireframes (Sketch)
  • Client presentation

With this work, GoGram is currently developing the app.

Osimfy

User Experience Design, Responsive Web Design

Client: BokoEco Partners
Completed: June 2017
Team: Amanda Chan, UX Designer, Bob Nassiri, UX Designer, Erin Ramsperger, UX Designer

View the full case study:

Brief

BokoEco Partners is a social enterprise cleantech manufacturer that produces Osimfy, a probiotic based product that controls odours and repels fruit flies from organic waste (compost) and alcohol. The website is currently a landing page, but BokoEco has requested a promotional website that showcases the many benefits of the Osimfy family of products and the ability to offer the products through an online shop.

Goals

  • Communicate the unique selling proposition of product to drive sales
  • Develop online shop for the product
  • Confirm their target market

Skills

User Research
User Interviews
Information Architecture
Prototyping (Paper & Digital)
User Testing

Research

With little content on the current landing page, we had to start from scratch. We spent time researching to understand the company and its’ products, the domain and any competitors, and the users. I focused on interviewing current users with Erin to understand their needs and motivations. Together we took the data from the interviews and survey to create an affinity diagram to inform the development of our user persona. A top concern we discovered for many users was the use of toxic chemicals for cleaning (Osimfy is non-toxic) and the effectiveness of an alternative. Read more about additional discoveries we found through our research in my case study. With this information and other findings we created our persona, Bill. Bill was used to guide our design of the website.

Design & Testing

My team did a design studio and generated a number of ways on how we could display the information in an engaging yet informative way. We used the mobile-first approach to develop the designs for this responsive website. Through collaboration and discussions we were able to agree on a single design to prototype and test. I further refined the sketches into our first paper prototype. With 4 rounds of testing on 14 different target users we were able to solidify the user flow and site map, develop wireframes and create a clickable mid-fidelity prototype.

Deliverables

  • Website copy
  • Persona
  • Medium-fidelity prototype (InVision)
  • Digital wireframes (Sketch)
  • Client presentation

Beedie Innovation Conference

Project Management

Client: CPA Innovation Centres
May - September 2016

Brief

Once a year, the Beedie School of Business’s CPA Innovation Centre hosts a full-day conference about innovation for Simon Fraser University faculty and students, and the public. Throughout the day, a professor from abroad, and Beedie School of Business faculty and PhD students present their work. The day concludes with a networking social.
As the sole event coordinator, I planned many details of the event (e.g., promotions, catering, and budgeting), maintained the registration, liaised with the speakers, and monitored and controlled the work of the marketing and building services teams.

Planning

I connected with the Centre Director to understand his vision for the conference. I asked questions to gather the requirements and understand the scope. What are the preferred dates? Where should it be located? What is the budget? Who is the audience? I then created a comprehensive list of required resources and tasks for the event (before, during, and after). I used backwards planning to create a timeline based on existing deadlines. From there I created a checklist of when everything needed to be done.

Executing, Monitoring & Controlling

I closely followed the timeline I created. When I had additional time, I worked ahead of schedule and prepare anything I could in advance. I worked closely with the marketing team to have them create the materials to promote the event: communicating deadlines, providing content, and giving feedback for any changes required. I also facilitated the out-of-town professor's visit by gathering their information and arranging the necessary documents for their travel and reimbursement of expenses.
Leading up to the event, I monitored the changing number of attendees and updated all affected areas. From room layouts to catering, I continued to make adjustments to maximize our resources. A final update was done once I had sent out a reminder email for the event as I anticipated cancellations.
During the event, I tracked attendees and monitored the progress of the presentations to make adjustments to the schedule. For example, when presenters ended early, I notified catering to move up the serving time for lunch.

Closing Process

Following the event, I compiled all the information I tracked and submit the updated budget and attendee list to the Director. Tracking information allows us to make better informed decisions for future events.

Target Audience
The demographic of people most likely to be interested in your product/service.

Cognitive Load
The amount of mental effort being used in a person's working memory.

Onboarding
The process of getting someone "up and running" with your site, app or service.

Tree Testing
Technique to evaluate the findability of topics in a website. Also known as reverse card sorting.

Cloud-Based
Ability to host a software platform or service from a remote location that can be freely accessed anywhere via internet.

Answer
A reaction to a question, statement or solution.

Interview Script
A document outlining the questions to be asked in a one-on-one conversation.
